About Meagan Klein

A native of St. Louis, MO, Meagan Klein graduated from Mehlville High School in 2005 and enlisted in the U.S. Navy in 2006 after completing one year at Saint Louis University. Following Recruit Training Command, she reported to the Defense Information School at Ft. Meade, Md., where she graduated the Basic Mass Communication Specialist Course as honor graduate for the Basic Public Affairs Specialist – Writer and Basic Still Photography sections.

In October 2007, Klein reported to Patrol Squadron (VP) 47 in Kaneohe Bay, Hawaii, where she immediately deployed to Iraq as a member of the first full Naval command deployed in-country in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom. During the deployment, Klein earned her Enlisted Aviation Warfare Specialist qualification. In August 2008, Klein attended Naval Air Crew Candidate School and “SERE” School. In November, she completed her Naval Air Crew Warfare qualification and earned VP47 Junior Sailor of the Year before deploying to Okinawa, Japan, in support of Commander, Task Force 72 and U.S. 7th Fleet.

In February 2011, Klein was nominated for duty with the White House Communications Agency (WHCA), Visual Information Command in Washington, DC. In May, she attended the Digital Multimedia Course “C” school, where she earned the Electronic Imaging System Specialist NEC. During school, Klein was promoted to Petty Officer 1st Class. While at WHCA, Klein traveled extensively to as an Audiovisual Engineer for the President and Vice President of the United States. She earned her bachelor’s degree from Thomas Edison State University and was selected as WHCA Sailor of the Year in 2013. In 2015, she became the first active-duty, legacy Mass Communication Specialist selected for Chief Petty Officer.

Klein currently serves in the U.S. Navy Reserve as the Senior Enlisted Leader for Navy Public Affairs Support Element – Midwest in Chicago, Il. She resides in St. Louis, MO, where she works as a web developer for SAIC. Klein’s decorations include a Defense Meritorious Service Medal, Air Medal, Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal (four awards) and various other unit and campaign awards.
